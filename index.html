Selected Research & Projects

Under Review

A Socio-Cognitive Process Theory of Error Response

This dissertation develops a computational process theory of post-error response integrating identity control theory and self-presentation theory, modeling how social context shapes risk-taking after errors. I analyzed play-by-play behavior data from over 106,000 possessions across the 2023–2025 NCAA Division I basketball seasons using linear mixed models and a Bayesian state-space model. This operationalizes errors as negative shocks to a latent identity verification state, building a measurement framework linking cognitive processes to observable behavioral sequences (under review — print should be out soon).

Determining the Individual- and Team-Level Effects of Team Tenure and Team Stability

Representing my master's thesis, I used secondary data from over 200 urgent care center teams. This study adopted a social network perspective to find that teams with greater shared experience, measured by internal density, led to higher customer satisfaction and lower wait times for patients. The effects of being on a closely-knit team, measured by weighted clustering coefficient, on collaboration and turnover perceptions, were also analyzed.

The Effect of Multi-Leader Familiarity on Hiring Decisions and MTS Performance

This study examines internal versus external hiring effectiveness within multiteam systems (MTSs) using longitudinal archival data from over 3,000 NCAA football coaches. We developed a network-based measure of multi-leader familiarity, finding internal hires outperform external hires through increased MTS familiarity.

In Progress

Quantification as a Barrier to Thought in Organizational Psychology

This manuscript argues that quantification in IO psychology (and the psychological sciences more broadly) functions not just as a set of analytic tools but as an epistemic constraint on how scientists perceive, think about, and theorize phenomena they study. Drawing on the sociology of scientific knowledge, I argue that quantification carries implicit structural commitments — such as stable dimensions, interval-level measurement, and context-independent constructs — that become internalized and institutionalized as organizational phenomena themselves. Using cases from selection and organizational research, I show how this quantification shapes not just the connection between hypotheses and data but the backbone of the entire paradigm of thought in IO psychology.

Beyond the Place: Investigating the Distal Impacts of Weather and Reviewer-Host Gender on AirBnB Review Sentiment

A myriad of factors influence AirBnB reviews, and it's important to investigate which factors are due to the actual home and which are external factors unrelated to host or house quality. This project examines the effect of weather at the time of stay and host-reviewer gender similarity. Largely due to limitations of operationalizing stay quality through review sentiment, the study did not find weather or gender similarity to significantly influence reviews.
